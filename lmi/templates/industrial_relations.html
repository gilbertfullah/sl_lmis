Industrial Relations

Overview

Welcome to the Industrial Relations page. This page is designed to help you manage and keep track of all your industrial relations cases. It provides an easy-to-use form to create new cases and allows you to view an overview of all your cases.

Industrial relations refer to the management of relationships between employers, employees, and their representatives. It encompasses various aspects such as collective bargaining, labour law, dispute resolution, and workplace health and safety.

Industrial relations function is crucial in any labour market information system as it helps to promote good working relationships between employers and employees, and ensure that workers' rights are protected. It helps to prevent and resolve disputes that may arise in the workplace, which can affect productivity, morale, and ultimately the success of an organization.

Moreover, industrial relations function provides critical information about the labour market, including trends in employment, working conditions, and wages. It helps to identify areas where improvements are needed and to develop policies that support the growth and development of the labour market.

Overall, industrial relations function plays a vital role in the labour market information system, as it ensures that the labour market operates fairly and efficiently, and that the interests of both employers and employees are represented and protected.
